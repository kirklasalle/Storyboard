COLD OPEN

================================================================================

INT. WHITE HOUSE — SITUATION ROOM — NIGHT

SUPER: "FOUR YEARS AGO"

The room hums with controlled emergency. Aides move fast. GENERAL HAYES (60s, four stars, a face built for exactly this moment) stands before a screen showing satellite imagery. Around the table: CABINET OFFICIALS, INTELLIGENCE DIRECTORS — and one empty chair.

GENERAL HAYES
She's not here.

AIDE #1
She's on her way, sir. Seven minutes.

GENERAL HAYES
We don't have seven minutes.

He looks at the empty chair. The decision sits in it, waiting for someone with the authority to carry it.

AIDE #2
General. The window closes in four
minutes. After that, we can't —

The door OPENS. AMBASSADOR CLAIRE DEVEREUX (50s, the composure of someone who has been in worse rooms than this and knows it) enters at a controlled walk that is almost a run.

CLAIRE
I'm here.

She reads the room in one sweep. Moves directly to the empty chair.

CLAIRE (CONT'D)
Brief me in thirty seconds.

GENERAL HAYES
The asset is burned. The operation
is compromised. If we extract now,
we expose three allied networks.
If we don't, he's dead by morning.

A beat. Claire looks at the screen. The satellite image. One heat signature, isolated.

CLAIRE
What's his name?

General Hayes blinks. Nobody asks that.

GENERAL HAYES
Codename MERIDIAN. Real name is —

CLAIRE
Not the codename. His name.

GENERAL HAYES
(after a pause)
Thomas. Thomas Reyes.

Claire looks at the image for one more second.

CLAIRE
Extract him.

GENERAL HAYES
Ambassador, the exposure risk —

CLAIRE
I know the risk. Extract him. I'll
carry the exposure.

She meets every eye in the room. No one argues. She picks up the secure phone.

SMASH CUT TO:

EXT. SOMEWHERE IN EASTERN EUROPE — NIGHT (FOUR YEARS AGO)

A figure running across dark ground. The thud of boots. Behind him: lights, vehicles, the grinding machinery of a manhunt.

He doesn't look back. Looking back is for people who haven't decided yet.

The helicopter appears over the ridge. The figure stops. Looks up.

THOMAS REYES (40s, the face of a man who has been living someone else's life for so long he's forgotten which parts are real) watches the helicopter descend.

He made it.

He's safe.

He has no idea what he owes.

SMASH CUT TO BLACK.

ACT ONE
================================================================================

A-STORY

INT. STATE DEPARTMENT — CLAIRE'S OFFICE — PRESENT DAY

SUPER: "PRESENT DAY"

CLAIRE DEVEREUX — now SECRETARY OF STATE — stands at the window. The city behind her. She holds a single sheet of paper and does not read it. She has read it twelve times.

A knock.

CLAIRE
Come.

DEPUTY CHIEF OF STAFF MARCUS WEBB (35, the kind of ambitious that has learned to look like dedication) enters.

MARCUS
Your nine o'clock is here.

CLAIRE
Send them in.

She sets the paper face-down on the desk.

A man enters. She knows who it is before she turns around. She knows the footfall.

THOMAS REYES stands in the doorway. Older. The kind of older that comes from things, not time.

THOMAS
Madam Secretary.

CLAIRE
(turning)
You couldn't just call?

THOMAS
This isn't a phone call.

She gestures to a chair. He doesn't take it. She sits. He stands. The geometry of what they are to each other.

THOMAS (CONT'D)
Someone is selling MERIDIAN.
The operation. All of it. Assets,
networks, allied contacts, the
extraction. All of it. Complete
file. Going to auction in seventy-two hours.

CLAIRE
Who knows you're here?

THOMAS
No one I trust.

CLAIRE
That's not what I asked.

THOMAS
(the only smile in the scene)
I know.

A beat. She looks at him the way you look at the person who cost you something you don't regret losing.

CLAIRE
Sit down, Thomas.

He sits.

B-STORY

INT. METROPOLITAN POLICE — DETECTIVE DIVISION — MORNING

DETECTIVE YUSUF ADEYEMI (42, seen everything, still gets angry about it — a rare combination that makes him exceptional) stands at a murder board. One photograph. No name.

His PARTNER, DETECTIVE ROSE NAKAMURA (34, sharp, running on coffee and the particular fury of someone who keeps being right) reads from a file.

DETECTIVE NAKAMURA
John Doe. Found in the Anacostia
at 0400. Single GSW, professional
placement. No wallet. No phone.
No prints on record.

DETECTIVE ADEYEMI
That's three in six months. Same
placement, same river, same nothing.

DETECTIVE NAKAMURA
Forensics says the slug is military
spec. Not commercially available.

YUSUF looks at the photograph.

DETECTIVE ADEYEMI
(quietly, to himself)
Who are you?

INTERCUT WITH:

INT. STATE DEPARTMENT — CLAIRE'S OFFICE — SAME TIME

Thomas Reyes holds out a photograph. Claire looks at it.

THOMAS
His name was CARDINAL.
He was the first asset we burned
to protect MERIDIAN.

BACK TO SCENE:

INT. METROPOLITAN POLICE — DETECTIVE DIVISION — MORNING

Yusuf stares at the faceless photograph.

DETECTIVE NAKAMURA
(phone buzzing)
Hold on. It's the M.E.

She steps away. Yusuf leans forward. Studies the John Doe.

He doesn't know yet. But the look on his face says some part of him can feel the size of what he's standing next to.

ACT TWO
================================================================================

A-STORY — CONTINUED

INT. STATE DEPARTMENT — SECURE CONFERENCE ROOM — DAY

Claire, Thomas, Marcus, and a woman introduced only as SEVEN (30s, intelligence community — the kind of controlled that suggests something is being controlled). A screen. Documents. The file.

THOMAS
Whoever has the file is selling
to the highest bidder. Foreign
intelligence. Private sector.
Doesn't matter which. The result
is the same: everyone in MERIDIAN
is dead within a week of the sale.

CLAIRE
How many people?

THOMAS
Forty-three active. Eleven retired.
Four of us already know we're on the list.

CLAIRE
(looking at Seven)
Options.

SEVEN
We find who has the file.
We recover it or destroy it.
We do it in seventy-two hours.

CLAIRE
And if we can't find it?

Silence. Seven holds it exactly one beat too long.

CLAIRE (CONT'D)
(the answer to her own question)
We surface the operation ourselves.
Make the file worthless by telling
the story first.

THOMAS
Claire —

CLAIRE
If everything comes out on our terms,
in our words, with our context —
it's a scandal. If it comes out
through an auction — it's a massacre.

THOMAS
You'd be finished. Politically.
Everything you've built —

CLAIRE
Thomas. I've been carrying this
since the night I sent that helicopter.
I'm tired of the weight of it.
(beat)
Find out who has the file.
Give me seventy-two hours to decide.

DREAM SEQUENCE — CLAIRE

INT. SITUATION ROOM — FOUR YEARS AGO (DREAM)

The room again. But this time the chair isn't empty. Her younger self sits in it, and the younger Claire is looking at her, and the question in her face is not what do we do but what will this cost, and the answer is written in every year between the two of them.

END OF DREAM SEQUENCE

Claire stands at the window. Present. The city in the morning light.

Her phone buzzes. She reads the message. A name. An address.

She picks up her coat.

CLAIRE (V.O.)
The first lie in this kind of work
is always the same. You tell yourself
it's just this once. That the exception
is not the rule.
(beat)
By the time you understand that
you've been living in the exception
from the beginning, you're standing
at a window wondering how to tell
the truth about something that was
built on a lie, and whether the truth
is still worth anything after
you've held it this long.

INT. METROPOLITAN POLICE — DETECTIVE DIVISION — NIGHT

Yusuf alone. The murder board. He's added two more photographs. A pattern emerging that he can almost see.

His computer PINGS. An email. Unknown sender.

He opens it. Reads. His expression doesn't change, but something behind it does.

He prints a single page. Looks at it.

On the page: Thomas Reyes' real name. His codename. His connection to three John Does in the Anacostia River.

Yusuf picks up his phone.

DETECTIVE ADEYEMI
(into phone)
Rose. Come back in.
We're not going home tonight.

He pins the page to the board.

The board, for the first time, begins to make sense.

FADE TO BLACK.

SERIES BIBLE NOTE:
The Meridian Accord is designed as a 10-episode first season on a premium
streaming platform. TV-MA. Runtime 55-62 minutes per episode. The show
operates on two timelines simultaneously: the consequences of a covert
operation four years prior (A-story), and a present-day murder investigation
that is slowly mapping its way to the same truth (B-story). The timelines
converge in episode 6. Episodes 1-5 each end with one piece of connective
tissue that brings the two stories one step closer together.

SERIES REGULAR INTRODUCTIONS:
  CLAIRE DEVEREUX — Secretary of State, architect of MERIDIAN
  THOMAS REYES — The asset who was extracted, the debt that came with him
  DETECTIVE YUSUF ADEYEMI — Homicide, doesn't know what he's walked into

RECURRING:
  MARCUS WEBB — Deputy Chief, Claire's staff, loyalties unclear
  SEVEN — Intelligence, no last name on purpose
