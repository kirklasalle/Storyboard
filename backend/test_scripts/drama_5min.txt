INT. LIVING ROOM - DAY

Dust motes dance in the late afternoon sun. SARAH (60s) sits by the window, a photograph in her lap.

DAVID (30s) enters, carrying a box. He looks around the half-packed room.

DAVID
We should finish the kitchen, Mom. The movers will be here tomorrow.

SARAH
I remember when we bought this rug. Your father spent three months convincing me it was the right shade of blue.

DAVID
(softly)
I know, Mom. But the new place is nice. It has that garden you wanted.

SARAH
A garden. For what? To watch things grow and then die?

David sets the box down and sits next to her. He takes her hand.

DAVID
It’s not about dying. It’s about a fresh start. We can’t stay here forever.

SARAH
Why not? Everything I know is within these walls. Your height marks on the doorframe. The stain on the ceiling from that storm in ‘98. 

DAVID
We’ll take those with us. Not the walls, Sarah. The memories.

Sarah looks at the photograph. It’s she and her husband, young and laughing on a beach.

SARAH
(whispering) 
I’m scared I’ll forget him if I leave.

DAVID
You won’t. I won't let you. 

He stands up, offering his hand.

DAVID
Let’s go see the garden. Just once more before we pack the rest.

Sarah hesitates, then takes his hand. They walk toward the door.

EXT. BACKYARD - CONTINUOUS

The garden is overgrown, but beautiful in its neglect. A single ROSE BUSH stands in the center.

SARAH
He planted that for our anniversary.

DAVID
(beat)
Let’s take a cutting. We can plant it at the new place.

Sarah smiles for the first time. 

SARAH
Yes. Let’s do that.

FADE OUT.
