Title:      THE HOUR BEFORE DAWN
            A Short Documentary Film

Producer:   Storyboard AI Demo
Director:   TBD
Runtime:    Approx. 8 minutes
Draft:      July 3, 2026

================================================================================
MASTER CLASS: Every A/V format element demonstrated.
Subject: Emergency room nurses — the overnight shift at a public hospital.
The story is told through action and silence. The narration arrives
late, after the images have already made their argument.
================================================================================

PRODUCTION NOTES:
All interview footage: available light only, no supplemental lighting.
B-roll: documentary style, handheld, participant-unaware where possible.
Music: sparse, single instrument. Should feel earned, not decorative.
Lower thirds: appear and dissolve slowly. Never interrupt a held moment.

A/V FORMAT ELEMENT REFERENCE — COMPLETE INDEX
================================================================================

STRUCTURAL COLUMNS:
  TIMECODE       HH:MM:SS — running clock from FADE IN
  VIDEO          Visual direction: shot description, camera notes, on-screen
                 text, graphics, lower thirds, B-roll, inserts
  AUDIO          All audio: narration, sync sound, V.O., music, SFX, ambient

VIDEO ELEMENTS:
  Wide shot      Establishing, geographic, contextual
  Medium shot    Character in environment, relationship visible
  Close-up       Detail, face, specific object
  Insert         Specific object filling frame (chart, monitor, phone screen)
  B-Roll         Supplementary footage over narration — action, environment
  Cutaway        Brief departure from main scene — context, counterpoint
  Intercut       Alternating between two or more locations
  GRAPHIC        Full-screen text, logo, animation, map, chart
  LOWER THIRD    Name/title super in lower portion of frame
  Slow motion    Noted with (SLOW MO) in video column
  Time lapse     Noted with (TIME LAPSE) in video column

AUDIO ELEMENTS:
  NARRATOR (V.O.)   Off-screen narration — first or third person
  SYNC SOUND        Live audio from the scene — ambient, dialogue, action
  V.O.              Character voice over — heard but not seen
  O.S.              Off screen — character present but not visible
  INTERVIEW (sync)  Subject speaking to interviewer on camera
  AMBIENT           Environmental sound — room tone, city, nature
  SFX               Specific sound effect — door, machine, footstep
  MUSIC IN/OUT      Underscore — specify instrument, mood, tempo
  MUSIC STING       Short sharp musical accent
  MUSIC HOLD        Music sustained under silence
  MUSIC FADES       Gradual exit of music
  SILENCE           Deliberate absence of sound — a direction, not an absence

PACING DIRECTIONS:
  Hold X seconds    Specific duration for a static image
  (NO NARRATION)    Deliberate silence — let the image carry
  (Let breathe)     Don't cut — the moment is still developing

CRAFT PRINCIPLES:
  — Narration arrives after image has made its argument
  — Silence is a direction, not an oversight
  — B-roll should contradict, expand, or deepen — never merely illustrate
  — Lower thirds appear and dissolve — they never interrupt a held moment
  — The (NO NARRATION) direction is one of the most powerful in documentary
  — Music earns its presence — every entry and exit is a choice
