TEASER
================================================================================

INT. FEDERAL EVIDENCE ARCHIVE — WASHINGTON D.C. — NIGHT

A vast underground room lined with grey metal shelving. Case boxes
stretching to vanishing point. Motion-sensor lights click on in sequence
as SPECIAL AGENT NADIA BELL (38, meticulous, runs on three hours sleep and
institutional fury) walks the aisle.

She stops at a box marked: MERIDIAN / CLASSIFIED / DNE.

DNE. Do Not Enter. She opens it anyway.

Inside: one cassette tape. Old. The label reads a date — seventeen years
ago — and three words: LAST KNOWN LOCATION.

NADIA
(to herself)
Still here.

Her phone BUZZES. She answers.

NADIA (CONT'D)
Bell.

DIRECTOR HAYES (V.O.)
We've got a body in Portland. You
need to see the scene before we
process it.

NADIA
Why me specifically?

DIRECTOR HAYES (V.O.)
Because he left something addressed
to you. By name. And Nadia — this
man has been dead for eleven years.

Nadia looks at the cassette in her hand. She already knows.

NADIA
I'm on the next flight.

SMASH CUT TO TITLE: COLD MERIDIAN

================================================================================
ACT ONE
================================================================================

EXT. PORTLAND RESIDENTIAL STREET — MORNING

Police tape. Neighbors behind it, holding phones like cameras. NADIA
ducks under the tape, flashing her badge to the uniforms.

DETECTIVE CALLUM REYES (45, Portland PD, competent and already irritated
by federal presence) meets her at the door.

REYES
Agent Bell. Your office called ahead.
I've been told to extend every courtesy.

NADIA
I'm sure that was painful.

REYES
You have no idea.

INT. PORTLAND HOUSE — LIVING ROOM — CONTINUOUS

It looks like any other room except for one thing: every surface is covered
in HANDWRITTEN NOTES. Walls. Floor. Every piece of furniture covered in
paper. And all of it connects with red string to a single photograph at
the center.

The photograph is of NADIA BELL. Taken recently. Without her knowledge.

REYES
The deceased is identified as —

NADIA
Victor Crane.

REYES
(pause)
You knew him.

NADIA
He was my informant. Eleven years
ago. His death was confirmed.
I attended the funeral.

REYES
Well, whoever was in that coffin,
it wasn't him. Because he's been
living here for six months, and
three days ago, something got to
him before he could get to you.

Nadia walks to the center of the room. She studies the photograph of
herself. Behind it, partially hidden: a USB drive.

NADIA
Has this been logged?

REYES
Found it thirty seconds before you.

She takes it. He doesn't object. He's watching her face.

REYES (CONT'D)
Who was Victor Crane, Agent Bell?

NADIA
(a long moment)
He was the only witness to something
that was supposed to never have happened.

INT. FBI FIELD OFFICE — PORTLAND — AFTERNOON

Nadia at a laptop. The USB drive contents load. Files. Hundreds.
The oldest dated seventeen years ago.

Her PHONE buzzes. Unknown number. She answers.

VOICE (V.O.)
(electronically distorted)
He told you to wait for this call.
He said you'd know the question.

NADIA
What's still buried?

VOICE (V.O.)
Everything. And they know you have
the drive. You have twelve hours
before they move.

The line goes dead. Nadia stares at the screen.

On it: a list of names. She recognizes them all. Senate. Pentagon.
Three of them she has briefed personally.

NADIA
(very quietly)
Oh, Victor.

================================================================================
ACT TWO
================================================================================

INT. FBI FIELD OFFICE — PORTLAND — LATER

Nadia has built her own version of Crane's wall. Reyes stands back,
watching, half-impressed, half-alarmed.

REYES
How do you get from a dead informant
to a Senate intelligence committee?

NADIA
Victor was tracking a program that
doesn't officially exist. Has been
for seventeen years.

REYES
What kind of program?

NADIA
The kind that turns citizens into
assets. Without consent. Without
warrants. Without anything.

REYES
You're talking about —

NADIA
I'm talking about people who have
been living as somebody else for
decades. Because someone with a
budget and no oversight decided
they were useful.

Reyes absorbs this.

REYES
Are you sure you trust me?

NADIA
I'm sure I need someone who knows
Portland. And you're the least
compromised person in this building.

REYES
That's a very low bar.

NADIA
Welcome to federal law enforcement.

================================================================================
TAG
================================================================================

INT. NADIA'S HOTEL ROOM — NIGHT

Nadia sits in darkness. On her phone, the photograph from Crane's wall —
herself, unknowing, captured mid-stride on a Washington street.

She opens her laptop. Types into a search field: the name on Crane's
earliest document. The name that predates everything.

The search returns: NO RECORDS FOUND. QUERY FLAGGED.

Her screen goes black.

Then a message appears, text only, white on black:

"WE KNOW YOU HAVE IT. RETURN IT AND THIS ENDS.
KEEP IT AND YOU BECOME CRANE."

Nadia reads it twice. She closes the laptop. She plugs in the USB drive.

NADIA
(to the darkness)
Then I become Crane.

FADE TO BLACK.

END OF PILOT
