ECHOES IN THE STATIC
A Short Film — Episodic Series Format
"Episode 3: The Memory Keeper"

Format: Episodic Short / Web Series
Runtime: Approx. 12 minutes
Draft: July 3, 2026

================================================================================
PREVIOUSLY ON ECHOES IN THE STATIC:
ARIA, a sound archivist, discovered her late father's hidden recordings
contain more than music — they contain voices of people who have vanished.
Episode 2 ended with a phone ringing in a disconnected apartment.
================================================================================

COLD OPEN

INT. ARIA'S APARTMENT — 3:47 AM

ARIA OSEI (32, headphones perpetually around her neck, sleeps in
four-hour intervals) sits cross-legged on her bed. Laptop open.
Waveform visualization filling the screen.

She's found something in Recording 7-B that wasn't there before.

Or that she didn't hear before. The distinction matters.

She plays it again. Slows it down. Then slower. And then — there.
Beneath the music her father recorded in 1991, a voice. Whispering.
Her name.

ARIA
(very still)
That's not possible.

She picks up her phone. Calls a number she has memorized but never
dialed. It rings once.

MALE VOICE (V.O.)
You heard it.

ARIA
How do you know what I heard?

MALE VOICE (V.O.)
Because I put it there. Your father
asked me to. Fourteen years ago.
He said you'd call when you were ready.

ARIA
Who are you?

MALE VOICE (V.O.)
Someone who has been waiting for
this phone to ring for a very long time.

Beat. Aria's hand tightens on the phone.

ARIA
Give me an address.

================================================================================

ACT ONE

EXT. VINYL RECORD SHOP — MORNING — DOWNTOWN

Small. Hand-painted sign. The kind of place that requires knowing
it exists before you can find it. Aria stands outside, checking
the address against her phone. This is it.

INT. VINYL RECORD SHOP — CONTINUOUS

Floor-to-ceiling shelves. Listening stations. The smell of old
cardboard sleeves. At the back, behind a counter stacked with
crates: OLIVER STRAND (60s, white beard, the patience of someone
who has learned that things arrive when they're meant to).

He sees Aria and his face changes. Not surprise. Recognition.

OLIVER
You look exactly like your father
described.

ARIA
You knew my father.

OLIVER
We worked together. For many years.
In a capacity I was not supposed
to discuss until you appeared.

He places a wooden box on the counter. Old, brass hinges, locked.

OLIVER (CONT'D)
Your father was a sound archivist,
same as you. But he was archiving
something specific. Something
that needed to be preserved without
anyone knowing it was being preserved.

ARIA
What was he preserving?

OLIVER
The voices of people the government
decided it would be convenient if
they were forgotten.

================================================================================

ACT TWO

INT. BACK ROOM OF RECORD SHOP — LATER

A reel-to-reel machine. Professional grade. Oliver threads a tape
from the box. Aria puts on headphones.

What she hears: voices. Dozens. Each one recorded in secret. Each one
giving their name, their story, their location — and the name of whoever
wanted them silenced.

ARIA
(headphones off)
These recordings are evidence.

OLIVER
Were evidence. The cases were closed.
The people were... resolved.

ARIA
How many?

OLIVER
Over thirty years? Your father
counted two hundred and forty-seven.

Aria sits back. She thinks of her father. The quiet man who collected
sounds. Who taught her to listen. Who died of a heart attack at sixty-one.

ARIA
He hid them in the music.

OLIVER
He spent twenty years encoding them
into recordings of his own compositions.
Each recording you have contains
the voice of someone who needed
to be remembered.

ARIA
Why me? Why now?

OLIVER
Because the statute of limitations
on the last case expires in thirty days.
After that, no prosecution is possible.
And because your father believed that
the person who would know what to do
with this would be the person he'd
spent thirty years teaching to listen.

A long silence. Aria looks at the wooden box. All those voices. All
that weight.

ARIA
Where do I start?

OLIVER
(handing her a file)
Recording 1-A. The first one he ever made.
The name on it is the name of the
person responsible for all the others.

ARIA
(reading the name)
I know this person.

OLIVER
Yes. That's why your father waited
for you.

================================================================================

TAG

INT. ARIA'S APARTMENT — NIGHT

Aria has built a new wall. Not of photographs like a detective.
A wall of sound: waveform printouts, recording logs, names with
red thread connecting them. At the center: a single name.

She sits at her recording equipment. She puts on her headphones.
She presses record.

ARIA
(quietly, deliberately)
My name is Aria Osei. My father was
Marcus Osei, sound archivist.
This is Recording 248.

She takes a breath.

ARIA (CONT'D)
And I am not going to let anyone
forget what he remembered.

The waveform on her screen begins to move. She starts to speak.

FADE TO BLACK.

END OF EPISODE 3
