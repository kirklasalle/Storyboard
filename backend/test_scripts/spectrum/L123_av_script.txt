A/V SPECTRUM ANALYSIS:

Level 1 — Video describes. Audio explains. No relationship between them.
          The two columns might as well be separate documents.

Level 2 — Video and audio work together. B-roll is intentional.
          The empty stool appears. Interview subject has texture.
          Lower thirds are informative. Pacing is professional.

Level 3 — The two columns are in active conversation.
          SILENCE is a deliberate editorial instruction — "ten full seconds.
          Do not cut this short." The EDITOR note is part of the script.
          The camera has a relationship with the subject, not just access to them.
          The final image (coffee on the stool) is discussed in the director's note
          before it appears — the documentary's structure is pre-announced by the craft.
          The NOTE ON APPROACH is part of the script: a directorial philosophy
          that governs every subsequent choice.

The difference between Level 2 and Level 3 in documentary A/V writing
is not the facts you include.
It is the relationship you declare with your subject before you press record.
