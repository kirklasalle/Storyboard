STORYBOARD AI — QUALITY SPECTRUM: LEVEL 2 (PROFESSIONAL)
Format: Plain Text Screenplay
Story: "The Witness" — A detective questions a diner waitress who saw a murder.

Level 2 is professional working-writer quality. Competent craft. The scene
moves, breathes, and has shape. Characters have voices. There is subtext.
This gets produced. It does not get remembered.

================================================================================

FADE IN:

INT. ALL-NIGHT DINER — 3 A.M.

The kind of place that survives on insomnia and cheap coffee. DETECTIVE HARRIS (50s, twenty-six years on the job, still takes notes by hand) sits across from MARY CHEN (40s, uniform, the practiced stillness of someone who has learned to wait things out).

A half-eaten slice of pie sits untouched between them. Neither ordered it.

HARRIS
You've been on since six?

MARY
Since five. We were short-staffed.

HARRIS
Long shift.

MARY
They're all long.

Harris opens his notebook. Studies something already written there.

HARRIS
The man who ran out. You told the
first officer he was wearing a jacket.

MARY
Black. Leather. He left in a hurry.

HARRIS
Did he pay?

MARY
No.

HARRIS
But you didn't call it in.

A beat. The kind of beat that has weight.

MARY
People walk out on checks sometimes.
I didn't know what had happened outside.

HARRIS
(not looking up)
But you knew something had happened.

Mary refills his coffee without being asked. Her hands are steady. Harris watches them.

HARRIS (CONT'D)
You ever see him before?

MARY
I see a lot of people.

HARRIS
That's not what I asked.

She sets the coffee pot down. Meets his eyes for the first time.

MARY
No. I'd never seen him before.

The fluorescents hum. Outside, a police radio crackles in someone's car.

HARRIS
(standing, pocketing his notebook)
We'll probably need you to come in.
Look at some photographs.

MARY
I understand.

He leaves money on the table — more than the coffee costs. Mary watches him go, then looks down at the exact spot where the man in the black jacket was sitting two hours ago.

She picks up his cup. Carries it to the back.

FADE OUT.

================================================================================
LEVEL 2 ANALYSIS:

What works:
  - Specific physical details that carry meaning (the half-eaten pie, the steady hands)
  - Characters have distinct presence — Harris has a career, Mary has a strategy
  - Subtext is present and consistent (Mary is lying, we feel it)
  - The scene has shape: arrival, mounting pressure, departure, coda
  - "She refills his coffee without being asked" — behavior that reveals character
  - The ending image (carrying away the cup) does interpretive work

What could be stronger at Level 3:
  - The action lines are competent but not yet specific enough to be visual poetry
  - Harris and Mary are types who are becoming people, but not yet fully realized
  - The scene's thematic resonance (complicity, survival, the cost of silence)
    is implied but not yet embodied in the physical world
  - "The kind of place that survives on insomnia" — good, but the language
    could be more earned, more precise
