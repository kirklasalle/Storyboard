INT. DINER - NIGHT

The clock over the counter TICKS. It’s 3:00 AM. 

ELIAS (40s, hat low, cigarette unlit) sits in a booth, staring at a damp envelope.

The bell rings. MARLOWE (30s, trench coat, eyes like flint) walks in. She sits across from him.

MARLOWE
You shouldn't have come back, Elias.

ELIAS
The money was gone. I had to.

MARLOWE
It’s never just the money with you.

She slides a matchbook across the table. Inside, a phone number is scrawled in red ink.

ELIAS
Whose?

MARLOWE
Someone who knows why you were set up.

(beat)

But they don't talk to ghosts.

Elias looks at the matchbook, then at Marlowe. The tension is a physical weight.

ELIAS
I’m not a ghost yet.

MARLOWE
Give it ten minutes. The heavy lifters are on their way.

She stands up and walks out. Elias is alone again. He strikes the match, lighting the matchbook. It flares up, a small pyre on the table.

He watches it burn.

EXT. ALLEY - CONTINUOUS

Marlowe steps into the shadow of the alley. A low-slung BLACK SEDAN waits. The window rolls down.

DRIVER (O.S.)
Did he take it?

MARLOWE
He took it.

DRIVER (O.S.)
Then he's already dead.

Marlowe watches the sedan pull away. She pulls her collar up.

FADE OUT.
